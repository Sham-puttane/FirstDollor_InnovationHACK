What are the different types of insurance?
Written by
State Farm®
Editorial Team
You've probably heard of car insurance, homeowners insurance and life insurance. Learn about coverage options for these and other types of policies.
Insurance provides valuable protection for you and your family. Choosing the right type and amount of insurance depends on your needs. Here you’ll find some of the more common types of insurance along with a short description of each, a few examples of available coverages, and resources where you can find more detailed information on each.
Property insurance
Homeowners insurance
Homeowners insurance
provides coverage for your home and other personal property in the case of a covered loss, such as a fire. Knowing about homeowners insurance and
what it covers
is important. It can also provide liability coverage in case you accidentally cause damage to someone else’s property or if someone gets injured at your home.
Renters insurance
Renters insurance
provides coverage for your stuff such as computers, TVs, electronics, bicycles, furniture and clothing in the case of a covered loss, such as a fire, theft or vandalism. It can also provide liability coverage in case you accidentally cause damage to someone else’s property or if someone gets injured at your apartment.
How much renters insurance to buy
depends on the value of your assets and what you need covered.
Condo insurance
Condo insurance
provides coverage for your condo and personal property in the case of a loss, such as a fire, theft or
other covered losses
. It can also provide liability coverage in case you accidentally cause damage to someone else’s property or if someone gets injured at your condo. Generally, condo associations only insure the building and common areas, leaving you responsible for your property.
Manufactured home insurance
Manufactured home insurance
provides coverage for your home and other personal property in the case of a covered loss. Your policy may vary depending on if you have a
manufactured or modular home
so it’s important to verify your coverage suits your needs.
Farm and ranch insurance
Farm and ranch insurance
provides coverage your dwelling and your household personal property like clothes, furniture, and appliances in your home. Your insurance can also cover farm buildings and farm personal property (such as farm machinery, grain, livestock and farm tools) that you use in your farming operations. You may want to explore the options of
scheduled or blanket coverage
for your farm personal property.
Flood insurance
Flood insurance
provides coverage for your home and other personal property in the event it gets damaged due to a flood. If you’re a homeowner, you may want to
prepare for a flood
and consider getting flood insurance since many homeowners insurance policies don’t cover damages caused by flooding. Even if you live in a moderate-to-low risk area, it can still be beneficial as those areas account for
about 40% of all flood claims
.
Earthquake insurance
Earthquake insurance
provides coverage for your home and personal property in the case of an earthquake. This type of coverage is usually excluded in a homeowners or renters policy but can be added through the purchase of an earthquake endorsement or a separate earthquake policy.
Vehicle insurance
Car insurance
Car insurance
provides coverage for your car if it gets damaged due to an event covered by your policy. It may cover things like an accident that involves another car, person, or even a thing. Car insurance can help protect you if you cause an accident. It may also help cover medical bills for you and your passengers if injured in an accident or if you’re hit by another driver who is either uninsured or underinsured. Car insurance may also provide you coverage for damage to property, theft, fire, vandalism and more so it’s important to
choose your coverages well
and review what your specific
state
requires.
Motorcycle insurance
Motorcycle insurance
provides coverage for your motorcycle, damage to property, injuries, theft, fire, vandalism and more. Learn more about
coverage options
and how your motorcycle premium is determined.
RV insurance
RV insurance
provides coverage for motorhomes, fifth wheels, toy haulers, travel trailers and camper trailers. Your policy can cover damage to property, injuries, theft, fire, vandalism and more. There are a
few things to know
when purchasing an RV.
Off-road vehicle insurance
Off-road vehicle insurance
provides coverage for off-road vehicles (including
snowmobiles
,
golf carts
and trail bikes, etc.), damage to property, injuries, theft, fire, vandalism and more. Along with proper coverage for your all-terrain vehicle (ATV), UTV or Side-by-Side, it’s important to follow
ATV safety tips
for a more safe and smooth ride. Depending on your state, your off road vehicle may need to be registered and titled. Check with your state’s Bureau of Motor Vehicle/Department of Motor Vehicle.
Boat insurance
Boat insurance
provides coverage for your boat, motor, trailer and boat equipment for loss caused by sinking, theft, fire, storms, collision and more. There is also
liability coverage
if your boat causes injury to others or damage to other boats.
Personal insurance
Life insurance
Life insurance
provides financial protection for your
beneficiaries
in the unfortunate event you pass. There are
many options to choose from
such as time frames (ranging from 10 years to end of life) and coverage amounts, just to name a few.
Health insurance
Health insurance
provides financial protection to help pay for costs associated with medical expenses. Health insurance can be complex, so it’s important to know
what it means
and the coverage you have. With the rising costs of health care and prescriptions, health insurance can help protect you from incurring debilitating bills as a result of a serious accident or illness.
Disability insurance
Disability insurance
helps provide many types of financial protection from an unexpected loss of income. The
many benefits
of disability insurance help you pay for things such as a mortgage, rent, a car loan and other regular living expense in case you become disabled and are unable to work.
Pet insurance
Trupanion® pet insurance
provides coverage that can help lessen the financial burden when it comes to medical care for your pet. Pet insurance can
cover your pet
for the long-term, as it often includes tests, surgeries and the treatment of breed-specific conditions.
Identity restoration insurance
Identity restoration insurance
provides coverage for things such as identity theft, computer attacks, cyber extortion and fraud, to name a few. Check out some tips on how to
help prevent identity theft
.
Personal liability umbrella insurance
Personal liability umbrella insurance
provides coverage for situations that exceed the limits of liability covered by an underlying policy such as car insurance, homeowners insurance or boat insurance.
Legal liability
can be the largest financial burden most individuals have. Umbrella insurance is meant to provide larger amounts of coverage for things included in the underlying policies and broader ranges of coverage for things not included in the underlying policies such as defamation of character, libel and slander.
Personal articles insurance
Personal articles insurance
provides coverage to repair or replace valuable items like a baseball card collection, camera equipment, fine art and jewelry, to name a few. Protection is usually broader than homeowners or renters insurance since those policies may have special dollar limits or only provide coverage for certain causes of loss.
Business insurance
Business insurance policies provide property and liability coverage for business owners. Different policies include
Business Owners Policies
(BOP),
workers’ compensation
,
commercial auto insurance
,
inland marine insurance
and
commercial liability umbrella policies
.
