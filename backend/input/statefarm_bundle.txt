Is bundling insurance worth it?
Written by
State Farm®
Editorial Team
Typically, yes. Bundling insurance policies may stack discounts and simplify policy management, saving you money and time.
Overview:
Bundling insurance policies with one provider may offer financial and practical benefits. By combining multiple policies, you can stack discounts and potentially save hundreds annually. Beyond cost savings, bundling provides digital convenience through unified billing, one login and streamlined claims processing. Whether you’re a new homeowner, a young professional or an expanding family, bundling simplifies insurance management while helping protect your possessions.
Bundling isn’t just industry jargon for “buying more.” It’s a strategic financial move. When you
bundle insurance

FAQs about bundling
Here are the answers to a couple more commonly asked questions.
Can I bundle if I already have an auto policy with State Farm?
Yes, you can. Call your agent to add home, renters or life insurance to your existing policy, and the multiline discount will apply.
Does filing a claim on my home affect my car insurance rates?
Generally, no. While both policies are with your insurance company, they are rated separately. A claim on your roof typically won’t spike the price of your auto insurance premium. Consider checking with your agent for more details.
